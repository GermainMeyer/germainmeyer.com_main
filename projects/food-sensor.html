Multimodal Soft Sensor to Assess Food Doneness

CREATE Lab, EPFL

October-December 2023

Humans perceive doneness based on numerous variables, from smells to colors and touch. This project asks how a robot can combine several of those signals to cook the perfect meal.

Approach

  • Combine several sensing modalities into a single robot fingertip form factor.
  • Mount the sensor on a robotic arm to use arm motion itself to enhance the modalities and differentiate textures.

Exploration of modalities

  • Stiffness, permittivity, conductivity, and temperature.
  • Vision and sound features including Laplacian, FFT, and PSD.

Design and fabrication

  • Combination of the most relevant modalities in a compact package.
  • Soft pressure sensor cast in silicone.

Arm-sensor combination

  • Sensor package mounted on a UR3 robotic arm.
  • Combination of arm movement and sensor modalities for greater distinction.

Results

  • Tested on toasts with different cooking times.
  • Clear distinction between cooking times with differences as small as 30 seconds.

Sensor used for Rapid Action Evaluation and Optimization. Paper presented at RoboSoft 2024 - DOI: 10.1109/RoboSoft60065.2024.10521945
