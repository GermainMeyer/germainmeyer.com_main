Vertical Farming Robot

CREATE Lab, EPFL - Research Fellowship

April 2025-Jan 2026

Plants go through multiple growth stages, each demanding unique interactions. An agricultural robot must adapt to these changes to enable autonomous crop management.

Approach

  • Vertical hydroponics enables space-efficient indoor farming, creating an ideal environment for robotic automation.
  • Use grippers adapted to each growth stage of the plant lifecycle: planting, monitoring, and harvesting.

Robotic base

  • 8 degrees of freedom.
  • Modular end effectors.

Combined system

  • Automatic hydroponic system.
  • Plant indexing using markers.
  • Computer vision for plant detection.

Teleoperation

  • For complex tasks.
  • Pathway for imitation learning.

Results

  • Precise planting and monitoring.
  • Successful harvesting of different plant types.

First author paper submitted to the International Conference on Intelligent Robots & Systems (IROS 2026).
